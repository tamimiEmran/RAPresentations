Results analysis

MNIST — near-trivial at ~98% with drops of 0.12–1.19 pp at 500 rounds. Even at 10% Byzantine the signal is too small to differentiate defences.

FEMNIST — moderate accuracy (86–87%) at 2000 rounds, but attack impact stays under 1.1 pp. The attack is barely visible.

CIFAR-10 + ResNet-18 — highest clean accuracy (68.67%) and a 16.98 pp attack drop (68.67% → 51.69%). Unambiguous signal. CIFAR-10 with MobileNetV3 / EfficientNet also shows 5–8 pp drops, so the dataset is genuinely hard — it is the architecture choice that separates the anchor from the secondary combos.

Observations

FedAvg grouping (k=10): clean accuracy drops 4.27 pp (68.67% → 64.40%). Under LF, grouped FedAvg (61.83%) outperforms ungrouped (51.69%) by 10.14 pp — grouping alone buys attack resilience at the cost of clean accuracy.

Krum — dramatic recovery with grouping: ungrouped Krum collapses at ~45% in both clean and attack settings because single-best gradient selection breaks down with 500 workers. Grouping rescues it: clean 66.40% (+21.29 pp), attack 69.44% (+24.52 pp). Grouped Krum is the highest-accuracy result in the phase — but only marginally beats the ungrouped-clean FedAvg target (+0.77 pp).

ShieldFL: stuck at 56–61% across all four cells. Grouping adds a small lift (+1.61 pp clean, +1.03 pp attack), neither breaks nor rescues the defence.

SignGuard: strongest ungrouped defence (68.17% clean, 63.12% attack). Grouping costs a marginal amount (−0.20 pp clean, −1.20 pp attack); grouped-best still reaches 69.80%.

Carry-forward decision
No baseline clears the "survives grouping and beats FedAvg under attack and approaches clean-FA accuracy" bar. Phase 4+ comparisons therefore reduce to FactorGraphs vs FedAvg only. The three collapsed defences are kept as context in the paper's Table 1 but not re-run in downstream sweeps.

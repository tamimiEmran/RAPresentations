Sample-Size Stability — Binary Balanced Accuracy

Shuffle the holdout, take the first n rows, recompute the metric. Median across 10 random seeds; bands show min–max.

10 seeds · n: 1 → 1M (log)

Takeaway: All four binary models reach their asymptotic balanced accuracy by n ≈ 1,000; the seed-to-seed range tightens to within ±0.015 by n ≈ 10,000 (~1% of the holdout) and within ±0.005 by n ≈ 100,000. A test set of ~10K rows already gives a reliable point estimate — the remaining ~99% of the holdout sharpens it but does not change the ranking of models.

Sample-Size Stability — Multiclass Macro-F1

Same sweep applied to the 13-class CatBoost model. Macro-F1 with zero_division=0 for unseen classes at small n.

10 seeds · n: 1 → 1M (log)

Takeaway: Macro-F1 climbs steeply through the small-n region because the metric needs at least one row per class to be well-defined — below n ≈ 30, several of the 13 classes are absent and contribute zero. The median crosses its asymptote (~0.88) by n ≈ 50, the inter-seed band tightens to ±0.02 by n ≈ 1,000, and to ±0.003 by n ≈ 100,000. So ~1K rows is enough to declare a stable macro-F1.

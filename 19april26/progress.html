Reviewer 2 — Comment 3

Intrinsic validation in unsupervised settings

Does the manuscript recommend only Jaccard-based sensitivity analysis, or a broader family of intrinsic validation metrics?

Section 3.3 positions Jaccard-based analysis as one instance of a broader family of intrinsic, label-free validation criteria. Clustering is covered by internal validity indices (Silhouette, Davies–Bouldin) and stability under perturbation; reconstruction-based detectors by the stability or separation of reconstruction-error distributions; threshold-based anomaly detection retains Jaccard as the worked example. Table 3's model-training row references intrinsic validation and sensitivity analysis matched to the task.

Reviewer 2 — Comment 4

User-level differential privacy for time series

Provide methodological guidance on user-level differential privacy in time-series smart-grid applications, and clarify the role of empirical privacy attacks.

The protected unit is defined as one consumer's full trajectory over the reporting window, not isolated readings. Guidance requires bounding each user's contribution before aggregation, adding noise to aggregated statistics or model updates rather than individual readings, and reporting the cumulative (ε, δ) loss when users participate across multiple rounds. Empirical attacks such as membership inference are framed as stress tests alongside the formal DP analysis, not as substitutes.

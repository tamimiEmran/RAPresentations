Takeaway: F1 saturates near 0.96 across all four models — a direct consequence of the 12:1 imbalance: recall ≥ 0.97 dominates and precision can't drop much (FP ≤ 97). On this dataset F1 does not discriminate; the comparison axes are specificity and balanced accuracy. On those, reasoning is the dominant lift: the 32B-Thinking variant is the only one that meaningfully recovers from the «always-manipulated» failure mode (specificity 0.454 vs ≤ 0.237 for the rest, Bal Acc 0.712 vs ≤ 0.613). At the small scale, 8B-Thinking ≈ 32B-Instruct on balanced accuracy (0.588 vs 0.613), confirming reasoning at 8B closes much of the gap to non-thinking 32B.

Takeaway: All four models share the same hard-class profile as the ML baselines — attack4, original, and attack1 are hardest because they involve shape-preserving or low-amplitude scaling. But the LLM ceiling on these classes is far below ML's: even the best Qwen3-VL-32B-Thinking-FP8 hits only 0.18 F1 on attack4 (vs CatBoost 0.72), and its overall macro-F1 (0.523) is well below CatBoost's (0.881). The instruct variants collapse on the hard classes entirely; only attack0 and attack9 (visually distinctive low-magnitude attacks) remain reliably resolved at 8B-Instruct, while attackieee drops to 0.42 because the always-attack4 bias confuses it with the shape-preserving family.

Takeaway: LLM specificity degrades far less across CV quartiles than ML did (~17 pp Q1→Q4 drop for 32B-T8 vs ~34 pp for CatBoost / ~47 pp for RF). The interpretation: LLMs read chart shape, not statistical features, so high-CV consumers don't trigger false positives the same way. But the LLM ceiling is much lower — 32B-T8's best-quartile 0.55 is below ML's worst-quartile 0.58 (CatBoost Q4). Less brittle, but uniformly weaker.

Takeaway: Reasoning costs an order of magnitude in latency. The 32B-Thinking variant — which is the only one with usable specificity — needs ~51 hr of single-GPU H100 time to score the 1,261-row subset; the same-quality multiclass story from 8B-Thinking lands in ~13 hr. The Instruct variants finish in 1–3 hr but with the always-manipulated bias intact. Practical takeaway: 8B-Thinking is the deployable point on this Pareto curve unless 32B's extra specificity is mission-critical.

Takeaway: Reasoning consistently helps at both scales — every accuracy metric is better, no metric collapses.
